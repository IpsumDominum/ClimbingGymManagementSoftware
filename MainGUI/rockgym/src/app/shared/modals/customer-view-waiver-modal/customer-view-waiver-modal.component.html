Before using the facility, it is important that each person is aware that there are risks associated with bouldering and associated activities. The risks include but are not limited to:

  • Falling to the ground or onto other users
  • Being fallen on by other users
  • Sports injury and over-exertion
  • Abrasions from the holds, walls, or the floor
  • Equipment failure
  • Injury through improper use of training and fitness equipment
  • Other users not following the Conditions of Use

    • The risks of participating in bouldering and related activities may result in serious neck and spinal injuries (which may result in paralysis), serious injury or impairment to other aspects of the body or even death.


    Conditions of Use:


    General

  • All customers and visitors must check in at reception on arrival.
  • If you are injured or witness an injury or unsafe or inappropriate behaviour please inform staff immediately.
  • Customers and visitors must not participate in or supervise any climbing or related activities whilst under the influence of alcohol, drugs, or other substances.
  • Staff will ask anyone acting inappropriately or unsafely to leave the premises

    • Bouldering:

    All first time or inexperienced climbers will be required to complete a safety induction with a staff member prior to climbing.

  • All experienced climbers will be asked a short series of questions to demonstrate competence. 
  • All under 18s must have their safety agreement signed by a parent or guardian.
  • Users under the age of 13 must be directly supervised by an experienced adult. This means child and adult being on the matted area together within clear sight of one another.
  • Adults may supervise up to 3 under 13s providing the adult is an experienced, registered climber or has completed an induction with a staff member.
  • Under 5s must be under constant close adult supervision. This means to be in arms reach of the child at all times. During peak times we may request this does not exceed a 1:1 ratio.
  • Soft mats do not guarantee safety, the safety descent method is down climbing.
  • Any fall may cause injury to yourself or others, please exercise caution at all times.
  • Holds may spin or break, if this happens please inform a staff member.
  • Please keep personal belongings off the mats, cubbies and lockers are provided.
  • Keep the fall zone clear. Be aware of other climbers, and do not go underneath or over another climber. 

    • Training and Fitness:

  • Please observe the safety signage located alongside the training and fitness equipment.
  • The equipment may not be used by under 18s unless supervised or authorised by an approved coach. 
  • Only use training and fitness equipment within your capabilities.
